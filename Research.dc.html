Research. 연구주제

We develop end-to-end theories and computational systems that connect quantum and atomistic information and curated materials data to multicomponent adsorption and cyclic separation performance. Our core research links molecular-scale behavior to material- and process-level outcomes, while data-driven discovery and AI scientist systems operate across the entire workflow to enable autonomous scientific reasoning.

01

Quantum & Atomistic Modeling

양자·원자 수준 모델링

We use quantum calculations, Monte Carlo methods, molecular dynamics, and enhanced sampling to investigate structure, energetics, diffusion, reaction, and electrochemical behavior at molecular scales.

Density Functional TheoryMonte Carlo MethodsMolecular DynamicsEnhanced Sampling
02

Adsorption & Statistical Mechanics

흡착과 통계역학

We develop statistical-mechanical theories and molecular simulations for multicomponent adsorption and confined diffusion, connecting molecular interactions to material-level performance.

Grand Canonical Monte CarloMulticomponent AdsorptionConfined Diffusion
03

Cyclic Swing Adsorption

순환식 스윙 흡착

We develop end-to-end models for pressure-, vacuum-, and temperature-swing adsorption that connect mixture adsorption and transport to cycle dynamics, process optimization, energy use, purity, recovery, productivity, and techno-economic performance.

Pressure Swing AdsorptionVacuum Swing AdsorptionTemperature Swing AdsorptionProcess OptimizationTechno-Economic Analysis

04

Data-driven & Autonomous Discovery

데이터 기반 자율 발견

We curate reproducible materials data and develop machine-learning models and high-throughput screening systems that identify, validate, and prioritize materials for targeted applications. These data and models provide the foundation for autonomous discovery.

Data CurationMachine LearningHigh-Throughput ScreeningMaterials Databases
05 · Emerging direction

AI Scientists & Computational Research Automation

AI 과학자와 계산 연구 자동화

We are developing AI scientist systems that orchestrate large language models, domain models, simulation codes, databases, and verification tools to plan, execute, inspect, and refine computational research workflows. The goal is reproducible, traceable, and human-verifiable research automation.
